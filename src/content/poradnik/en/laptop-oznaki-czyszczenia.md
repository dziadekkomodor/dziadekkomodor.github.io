If your laptop is running hot, loud, or suddenly shutting down under load, the culprit is
usually clogged cooling and dried-out thermal paste — not a failing component. It's one of the
most common, and cheapest, problems we fix.

## Is your laptop running loudly?

If the fan spins at full speed even during ordinary browser use, that's a sign the cooling
system is working hard just to keep up with heat that isn't being carried away efficiently.

## Does the case get hot?

Heat around the keyboard or the underside of the case, especially after only a short time in
use, usually means heat isn't being effectively pushed out of the machine.

## Does your laptop suddenly shut down under load?

That's a thermal protection kicking in — the processor shuts itself down to avoid damage once
the temperature crosses a safe threshold. Worth acting on before it causes permanent damage to
components.

## Has performance dropped compared to when you bought it?

An overheating processor automatically throttles its own clock speed to protect itself — which
is why a laptop "feels slower than it used to," even though nothing about the hardware itself
has changed.

## When was the inside last cleaned?

If you can't remember, or it's been more than 1–2 years, that alone is reason enough to get it
checked — dust builds up gradually and invisibly.
